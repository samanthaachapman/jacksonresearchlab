Research Focus

The Jackson Research Lab centers on advancing mental health equity through trauma-informed, participatory, and relational methodologies. Our work seeks to reflect lived experiences and reimagine systems of care for youth and families navigating structural vulnerabilities.

Key Areas of Focus

We investigate the lived realities of young people engaged with mental health systems, with special attention to transitions in care, trauma-informed practice, and culturally safe services. Our research is grounded in narrative and arts-based methods that amplify youth voice and co-create meaningful change.

Methodologies

Our team applies qualitative, mixed-methods, and community-based participatory designs. We integrate feminist, Indigenous, and critical theoretical lenses in order to support ethical, equity-oriented, and compassionate inquiry.

Collaboration

We work with academic and community partners across disciplines and sectors including nursing, public health, social work, education, and digital health. Our partnerships prioritize reciprocity, inclusion, and long-term systems impact.
