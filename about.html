About the Jackson Research Lab

The Jackson Research Lab is dedicated to advancing mental health equity through trauma-informed, participatory, and narrative-based inquiry. Grounded in compassion and accountability, our research seeks to reflect lived experience, reimagine care systems, and strengthen the voices of youth and families navigating structural vulnerabilities.

Our Approach

Our work draws on qualitative and mixed methods with an emphasis on equity-oriented and relational approaches. We collaborate with community partners, youth, and families to co-create knowledge that supports more accessible and responsive care environments.

Led by Dr. Margot Jackson

Dr. Jackson is a professor at the University of Alberta in the Faculty of Nursing. Her work spans youth psychiatric care, Indigenous health, and trauma-informed systems. She brings over a decade of experience working with populations facing structural barriers and guiding interprofessional research teams.
